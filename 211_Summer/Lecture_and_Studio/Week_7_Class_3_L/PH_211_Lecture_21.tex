\underline{Prediction}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Experiment 1 is conducted with two carts, $\text{A}_{1}$ and $\text{B}_{1}$, on a level, frictionless track. The mass of cart $\text{B}_{1}$ is greater than that of cart $\text{A}_{1}$ (\textit{i.e.} $m_{\text{B}_{1}} > m_{\text{A}_{1}}$).
	\item In experiment 1, cart $\text{A}_{1}$ moves toward cart $\text{B}_{1}$, which is initially at rest. Magnets are attached to the carts so that the carts repel each other without touching. After the collision, cart $\text{A}_{1}$ has reversed direction and cart $\text{B}_{1}$ moves to the right.
	\item \textit{Predict} whether the magnitude of the final momentum of cart $\text{B}_{1}$ is \textit{greater than}, \textit{less than}, or \textit{equal to} that of the system $\text{S}_{1}$ of both carts. Briefly explain.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Main Ideas}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Momentum and impulse are useful quantities for solving dynamics problems.
	\item The impulse is always equal to the change in momentum for a system.
	\item When the impulse is zero (because the net force is zero), the momentum of the system is constant---it is \textit{conserved}.
\end{itemize}
